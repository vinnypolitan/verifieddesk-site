A lead list where every row tells you how much to trust it

Most lists hide their weak rows. Ours label them. Every contact ships with a verification grade, and anything that fails all checks is removed and disclosed, not padded in to hit a row count.

The grading system

Three tiers, priced by confidence

You should not pay confirmed rates for guessed data. Contacts are graded by how far verification got, and packages are priced accordingly.

TIER A / VERIFIED

Confirmed deliverable

The mailbox was checked and confirmed to accept mail. Named contact, role, and company, ready for outreach.

TIER B / PATTERN

Pattern matched

The domain accepts mail but will not confirm individual mailboxes (a catch-all server). The address follows the company's confirmed email pattern.

TIER C / DIRECTORY

Directory grade

Confirmed company record without a verified individual contact. Useful for phone or form outreach and for market mapping.

Records that fail every check are dropped from the file and listed in the disclosure note with a reason. That number is never hidden.

What you receive

The deliverable

  1. The list

    XLSX and CSV. Company, contact name, role, email with tier grade, phone where available, and a source note on every row.

  2. The disclosure note

    How many records were considered, how many shipped in each tier, how many were dropped and why.

  3. The method note

    Which sources were used and how contacts were verified, in plain language, so your team can judge the data.
