How a delivery works

Five steps, one disclosure note

  1. Brief

    You describe the buyer or the question. We confirm scope, price, and deadline in writing before anything starts.

  2. Build

    We pull from public registries, directories, filings, and company sites. Every row and every claim keeps its source.

  3. Verify

    Contacts are checked and graded. Claims are traced back to a named source or cut.

  4. Disclose

    Anything that failed verification is removed and listed in a disclosure note, with the reason. You see what was cut, not just what survived.

  5. Deliver

    Files land in your inbox in the format you asked for. Revisions within scope are included.

Common questions

Asked before hiring us

What does verified actually mean?

Every contact is checked before it ships and labeled with one of three grades. Tier A means the mailbox was confirmed deliverable. Tier B means the domain accepts mail but will not confirm individual mailboxes, so the address is pattern matched from confirmed company data. Tier C means directory grade company data without an individual contact. Records that fail all checks are removed and disclosed.

What happens to contacts you cannot verify?

They are dropped from the deliverable and disclosed. Each delivery includes a note listing how many records were removed and why. You are never charged verified rates for unconfirmed data.

Where does the data come from?

Public business sources: government registries, licensing bodies, trade directories, company websites, and public filings. Every row carries a source note so you can see where it came from.

How fast is delivery?

Most lead list packages ship in 2 to 5 business days. Research reports ship in 3 to 7 business days depending on scope. A free sample usually ships within 24 hours.
